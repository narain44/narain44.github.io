Perfect 1-Day NYC Itinerary

8:00 AM - 9:00 AM
Times Square
Start your day in the heart of NYC. Experience the iconic billboards and energy of Times Square. Perfect for photos and people-watching.
View on Google Maps
9:30 AM - 11:00 AM
Top of the Rock Observation Deck
Head to Rockefeller Center for breathtaking 360-degree views of NYC from the Top of the Rock. See Central Park, Empire State Building, and more.
View on Google Maps
11:30 AM - 1:00 PM
Central Park
Stroll through Central Park, visit Bethesda Terrace, and enjoy the peaceful oasis in the middle of the city. Perfect for a light lunch at one of the park's cafes.
View on Google Maps
1:30 PM - 3:00 PM
The Metropolitan Museum of Art
Explore one of the world's greatest art museums. Must-see exhibits include the Egyptian Temple of Dendur and European paintings.
View on Google Maps
3:30 PM - 5:00 PM
Fifth Avenue Shopping
Walk down Fifth Avenue for some window shopping or actual shopping at iconic stores like Tiffany & Co., Saks Fifth Avenue, and more.
View on Google Maps
5:30 PM - 7:00 PM
Empire State Building
Image by King of Hearts, CC BY-SA 4.0
Visit the Empire State Building for sunset views of the city. The observation deck offers stunning panoramic views.
View on Google Maps
From Fifth Avenue: Take N Q R W to 34th St-Herald Square (2 stops, 5 minutes) or walk 15 minutes
7:30 PM - 9:00 PM
Dinner in Midtown
Image by Diliff, CC BY-SA 3.0
Enjoy dinner at one of NYC's famous restaurants. Options include:
  • Carmine's (Italian family-style dining)
  • Keens Steakhouse (Classic NYC steakhouse)
  • Junior's Restaurant (Famous for cheesecake)
From Empire State Building: Most restaurants are within walking distance (5-10 minutes)
9:30 PM - 11:00 PM
Broadway Show
Image by Ajay Suresh, CC BY 2.0
End your day with a Broadway show. Popular current shows include:
  • The Lion King
  • Hamilton
  • Wicked
From Dinner: Most theaters are within walking distance (5-15 minutes) or take 1 2 3 to Times Square-42nd St (1 stop, 3 minutes)

Travel Tips

  • Purchase a MetroCard for easy subway access (costs $2.90 per ride)
  • Consider getting an Unlimited MetroCard if taking multiple rides
  • Download the MTA Subway Time app for real-time train arrivals
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes
  • Book Broadway tickets in advance
  • Consider getting a CityPASS for discounted attraction entry
  • Check the weather forecast and dress accordingly
  • Subway trains run 24/7, but frequency decreases after midnight
